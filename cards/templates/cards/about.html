MOWIN -
An Anishinaabemowin Vocabulary App

Anishinaabemowin Language

Anishinaabemowin, also known as Ojibwe, is one of the largest Indigenous languages north of Mexico. It is the ancestral language of the Anishinaabe people and is spoken across parts of Canada and the United States, particularly around the Great Lakes region. As a living language that carries centuries of cultural knowledge and wisdom, it continues to be vital to Anishinaabe identity and way of life.

The Leitner Learning System

MOWIN uses the Leitner system, a scientifically proven method of spaced repetition learning. This system was developed by Sebastian Leitner in the 1970s and is designed to help users learn more efficiently by focusing on the material they find most challenging.

How it Works:

  • Cards are sorted into different boxes based on how well you know them
  • Correctly answered cards move to the next box and are reviewed less frequently
  • Incorrect cards return to the first box for more frequent review
  • This system ensures you spend more time on challenging words and less time on ones you know well

Purpose

MOWIN is designed to support language learners in their journey to learn and practice Anishinaabemowin vocabulary. By combining traditional language learning with modern educational techniques, it aims to help contribute to the preservation and revitalization of this important Indigenous language.

This app is a work in progress and is not yet ready for public use.
